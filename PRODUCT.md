# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary user is an event guest completing a short, touch-first photo session on
a paired tablet. They are usually unfamiliar with the system, may be standing in
mixed or low event lighting, may be part of a group, and expect a clear path to a
physical print and private phone delivery.

The booth operator is a photographer or trained staff member who prepares the event,
monitors camera and printer readiness, handles payment or print exceptions, and
recovers interrupted sessions. The photography-business owner configures packages,
templates, staff, devices, retention, and operations. Our internal platform team
supports tenants, licensing, billing, security, and incidents.

The product UI is rendered as responsive web surfaces in a Windows WebView2 host and
in guest or owner browsers. The Windows booth engine remains the hardware authority.

## Product Purpose

DigiPhoto Booth System turns capture, layout, payment, printing, and private digital
delivery into one durable workflow. It exists so a photography business can operate
reliably at an event—even when the internet fails—and later run the same proven stack
as a turnkey multi-tenant SaaS product.

Success means guests complete sessions without instruction, operators can understand
and recover every exception, outputs are never lost or duplicated by software, and
owners can configure events without compromising payment, privacy, or print safety.

## Positioning

The photobooth that treats every guest session like a recoverable production job,
from shutter to print to private QR delivery.

## Brand Personality

Cozy, celebratory, precise.

Machi Studio (`まちスタジオ`) is the tenant-facing brand. Its capybara character,
soft blush palette, and keepsake-stationery cues make the guest experience warm
without making important controls childish or chatty. The operator and owner
experiences are calm, explicit, and trustworthy.
Copy uses short active sentences, names the real action, and becomes serious—not
humorous—during payment, privacy, printing, or recovery problems.

## Anti-references

- A casino-like vending kiosk full of flashing choices, countdown anxiety, and
  unclear payment states.
- A generic wedding collage app where pink gradients, script type, hearts, or mascot
  art compete with privacy, payment, capture, print, or recovery controls.
- A sci-fi dark control room that makes routine operator work feel dangerous or
  technical for its own sake.
- A Canva clone that exposes unrestricted editing to guests and hides print safety
  behind creative freedom.
- A generic SaaS dashboard made from equal cards, ornamental metrics, vague status
  colors, and decorative charts.
- A social feed or public event wall. Delivery is private by default.

## Design Principles

### One next action

Each guest screen answers three questions immediately: where am I, what happens now,
and what can I do next. Secondary actions never compete with the primary path.

### Show operational truth

Operator and owner surfaces expose the real state of the camera, printer, payment,
sync, storage, license, and session. Never turn an ambiguous state into a reassuring
success message.

### Recover without surprise

Every interruption has a safe next step. Preserve completed work, explain what will
and will not happen, and require explicit operator action before a risky retry.

### Creativity inside production boundaries

Staff can make distinctive event templates from defaults or a blank canvas, while
the system preserves immutable publication, safe areas, output dimensions, asset
integrity, and deterministic rendering. Guests choose bounded options instead of
editing the canvas.

### Privacy is part of the flow

Notice, guardian handling, private delivery, retention, revocation, and deletion are
visible product behaviors—not policy links added after the workflow is built.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for all web-rendered surfaces. Support keyboard and switch-style
navigation, visible focus, 200% zoom, screen-reader names and status announcements,
44px minimum touch targets, coarse and fine pointers, portrait and landscape tablet
use, and color-blind-safe states that never rely on color alone.

Respect reduced-motion preference everywhere. Avoid vestibular effects, looping
attention motion, and time pressure that cannot be extended. Privacy and guardian
notices use plain language; the minor flow includes a child-appropriate explanation.
The core guest path remains understandable in English-first version one and is
structured for later localization without text baked into imagery.
